Academic Status Policy

Academic Standing Classifications

Purpose

The Academic Status Policy defines the criteria for determining a student's academic standing and outlines the academic intervention processes for students who are not meeting academic performance standards.

Academic Status Categories

1. Good Standing

Criteria: Students meet or exceed minimum GPA requirements:

  • Undergraduate: Cumulative GPA ≥ 2.0
  • Graduate: Cumulative GPA ≥ 3.0

Students in Good Standing are eligible for registration, financial aid, and all College privileges.

2. Academic Warning

Criteria: Students fall below minimum GPA for the first time:

  • Undergraduate: Cumulative GPA < 2.0
  • Graduate: Cumulative GPA < 3.0

Consequences:

  • Mandatory academic advising meeting;
  • Development of an Academic Success Plan;
  • May be required to reduce course load;
  • Registration for the following term may be delayed untiladvising requirements are met.

3. Academic Probation

Criteria: Students on Academic Warning who fail to return to Good Standing after one semester.

Consequences:

  • Continued mandatory academic advising;
  • Required participation in academic support programs;
  • Course load may be limited;
  • May lose eligibility for certain scholarships;
  • Must achieve required GPA by end of probationary period to avoid suspension.

4. Academic Suspension

Criteria: Students on Academic Probation who fail to return to Good Standing after one additional semester.

Duration: One semester (Fall or Spring)

Consequences:

  • Student is not permitted to enroll for one semester;
  • Financial aid eligibility may be affected;
  • Student may petition for reinstatement after suspension period;
  • Must demonstrate readiness to return (may require coursework at another institution or evidence of changed circumstances).

5. Academic Dismissal

Criteria: Students who return from Academic Suspension and fail to achieve Good Standing, or students with a term GPA < 1.0.

Duration: Permanent separation from the College

Appeal: Students may appeal dismissal through the Academic Appeals process.

Dean's List and Honors

Dean's List: Undergraduate students who earn a term GPA of 3.5 or higher while enrolled in at least 12 credits.

President's List: Undergraduate students who earn a term GPA of 3.8 or higher while enrolled in at least 12 credits.

Appeal Process

Students may appeal Academic Suspension or Dismissal by submitting a written petition to the Academic Standards Committee within 10 business days of notification.

Reinstatement After Suspension

Students seeking reinstatement must:

  • Submit a reinstatement petition;
  • Provide evidence of readiness for academic success;
  • Meet with an Academic Advisor to develop an Academic Success Plan;
  • Agree to conditions of return (e.g., reduced course load, mandatory tutoring).
