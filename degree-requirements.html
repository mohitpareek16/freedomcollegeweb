Degree Requirements

Academic Program Standards

Overview

All degrees at Freedom College of Excellence require completion of specific academic requirements including general education, major coursework, electives, and minimum credit hours.

Associate Degree Requirements

Total Credits: 60 Semester Hours

Requirement Category Credit Hours
General Education Core 18-24 credits
Major/Program Courses 24-30 credits
Electives 12-18 credits
Total 60 credits

Minimum GPA: 2.0 cumulative
Residency Requirement: Minimum 15 credits at Freedom College
Time Limit: Must be completed within 6 years of initial enrollment

Bachelor's Degree Requirements

Total Credits: 120 Semester Hours

Requirement Category Credit Hours
General Education Core 36-42 credits
Major Requirements 36-48 credits
Minor (optional) 18-21 credits
Electives 24-36 credits
Total 120 credits

Minimum GPA: 2.0 cumulative; 2.5 in major
Residency Requirement: Minimum 30 credits at Freedom College, including at least 15 credits in the major
Upper-Level Coursework: Minimum 45 credits at 300-400 level
Time Limit: Must be completed within 10 years of initial enrollment

Master's Degree Requirements

Total Credits: 30-36 Semester Hours (program-specific)

Requirement Category Credit Hours
Core Courses 15-18 credits
Concentration/Electives 12-15 credits
Capstone (Thesis or Project) 3-6 credits
Total 30-36 credits

Minimum GPA: 3.0 cumulative
Residency Requirement: Minimum 75% of credits at Freedom College
Capstone Requirement: Thesis, comprehensive exam, or capstone project
Time Limit: Must be completed within 5 years of initial enrollment

Common Degree Components

1. General Education Core

All undergraduate degrees require completion of general education courses covering communication, mathematics, sciences, social sciences, humanities, and global awareness. See the General Education Requirements policy for details.

2. Major Requirements

Each major has specific required courses, typically 36-48 credits for bachelor's degrees. Students must achieve a minimum 2.5 GPA in major coursework.

3. Minor Requirements (Optional)

Undergraduates may declare a minor, requiring 18-21 credits in a secondary field of study. A minimum 2.0 GPA in the minor is required.

4. Electives

Elective credits allow students to explore interests, take additional courses in their major, or complete a second major or minor.

5. Capstone Experience

Many programs require a capstone course, thesis, comprehensive exam, internship, or senior project to integrate learning.

Course Level Distribution

Undergraduate:

  • 100-200 level (Lower Division): Foundational courses
  • 300-400 level (Upper Division): Advanced courses; minimum 45 credits required for bachelor's degrees

Graduate:

  • 500-600 level: Graduate-level courses

Degree Audit

Students can view their degree audit via the student portal to track completed requirements and plan remaining coursework. Students should meet with their academic advisor at least once per academic year.

Catalog Year

Students follow the degree requirements published in the academic catalog in effect at the time of their initial enrollment, provided they maintain continuous enrollment. Students who are absent for more than two consecutive semesters must follow the catalog in effect upon readmission.
