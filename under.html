Undergraduate Admissions Policy

Purpose

This policy outlines the principles, requirements, and procedures governing undergraduate admissions to Freedom College of Excellence. Our goal is to recruit, admit, and enroll a diverse and talented student body poised to contribute to and benefit from the College’s unique academic environment.

Policy Statement

Freedom College of Excellence is committed to a holistic review process that evaluates each applicant's academic achievements, intellectual curiosity, personal character, and potential for growth. We seek students who demonstrate a commitment to academic excellence, ethical leadership, and a spirit of inquiry, consistent with the College’s mission.

General Admissions Requirements

All applicants for undergraduate admission must meet the minimum requirements outlined below to be considered. For questions, please contact the Office of Undergraduate Admission.

Application Procedure

  1. 1

    Application Completion

    Submit the Freedom College Application or the Common Application online, including the application fee or fee waiver request.

  2. 2

    Transcript Requests

    Provide official transcripts from all secondary and post-secondary institutions attended.

  3. 3

    Entrance Evaluation

    Submission of SAT or ACT scores is optional but recommended. Our holistic review considers scores within the full context of an applicant's academic profile.

  4. 4

    High School Diploma/GED

    Applicants must have earned a diploma from an accredited high school or a General Educational Development (GED) certificate.

  5. 5

    Criminal Record Disclosure

    Applicants must disclose any prior felony convictions as part of the application process.

Equal Opportunity Admissions

Freedom College of Excellence admits students of any race, color, national and ethnic origin to all the rights, privileges, programs, and activities generally accorded or made available to students at the school. It does not discriminate on the basis of race, color, national and ethnic origin in administration of its educational policies, admissions policies, scholarship and loan programs, and athletic and other school-administered programs.

Homeschooled Applicants

We welcome applications from homeschooled students. In addition to the general requirements, homeschooled applicants should provide:

  • A detailed curriculum description and book list for grades 9-12.
  • A portfolio of representative academic work.
  • Official results from standardized tests (e.g., SAT, ACT, AP exams) to demonstrate subject mastery.
  • At least one letter of recommendation from a non-family member who can speak to the applicant’s academic and personal qualities.

Recordkeeping & Compliance

All application materials become the property of Freedom College of Excellence. The Office of Admissions is responsible for maintaining all undergraduate admission records in accordance with institutional policy and federal regulations.

Compliance References:

  • Title IV of the Higher Education Act
  • Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)
  • Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972
