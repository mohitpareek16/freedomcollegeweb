Academic Advising Policy

Supporting Student Success

Purpose

Academic advising is a collaborative partnership between students and advisors designed to support academic success, degree completion, and personal development. This policy outlines advising responsibilities, expectations, and procedures.

Advising Philosophy

Freedom College embraces a developmental advising model that goes beyond course selection to include:

  • Clarifying academic and career goals;
  • Developing critical thinking and decision-making skills;
  • Navigating college resources and policies;
  • Encouraging personal growth and self-advocacy.

Advisor Assignment

Undergraduate Students:

  • Undeclared Majors: Assigned to a professional advisor in the Academic Advising Center;
  • Declared Majors: Assigned to a faculty advisor within their major department;
  • Transfer Students: Assigned upon admission based on declared or intended major.

Graduate Students:

  • Assigned to a faculty advisor or program director within their graduate program upon admission.

Advisor Responsibilities

Academic advisors are expected to:

  • Meet with advisees at least once per semester;
  • Assist with course schedule planning and registration;
  • Monitor academic progress and degree requirements;
  • Refer students to appropriate campus resources (tutoring, counseling, career services);
  • Provide guidance on academic policies and procedures;
  • Help students explore academic and career interests;
  • Respond to student inquiries within 48 hours (during business days).

Student Responsibilities

Students are expected to:

  • Schedule and attend advising appointments each semester before registration;
  • Come prepared with questions, degree audit, and course preferences;
  • Monitor their own degree progress through the student portal;
  • Understand and comply with academic policies and deadlines;
  • Communicate changes in academic goals, major, or personal circumstances;
  • Take ownership of their academic decisions and success.

Mandatory Advising Requirements

Advising is mandatory for:

  • First-Year Students: Must meet with advisor before first registration and at least once per semester during the first year;
  • Students on Academic Warning/Probation: Required to meet with advisor and develop an Academic Success Plan;
  • Students Changing Majors: Must meet with both current and new major advisors;
  • Students Applying for Graduation: Must meet with advisor to verify degree completion.

Registration Hold: Students who do not complete mandatory advising may be placed on a registration hold.

Advising Appointments

How to Schedule:

  • Online through the student portal;
  • Email your assigned advisor;
  • Walk-in hours (if available, check department website).

Appointment Types:

  • Registration/Course Planning: 20-30 minutes
  • Major/Career Exploration: 30-45 minutes
  • Academic Difficulty/Success Planning: 30-45 minutes
  • Graduation Planning: 30-45 minutes

Peak Advising Periods: Registration periods (March-April for Fall; October-November for Spring). Schedule early to avoid delays.

Degree Audit

Students should review their degree audit regularly through the student portal. The degree audit shows:

  • Completed requirements;
  • In-progress coursework;
  • Outstanding requirements for graduation.

Responsibility: While advisors assist with interpretation, students are ultimately responsible for understanding their degree requirements.

Changing Advisors

Students may request a change of advisor by:

  1. Contacting the Academic Advising Center (for undeclared majors) or department chair (for declared majors);
  2. Explaining the reason for the requested change;
  3. Awaiting confirmation of the new advisor assignment.

Advisor changes are made to improve the advising relationship and are granted when possible.

Advising for Special Populations

Student-Athletes

Work with both an academic advisor and an athletic academic coordinator to ensure NCAA compliance and eligibility.

Transfer Students

Receive specialized advising to evaluate transfer credits, plan remaining requirements, and ensure timely degree completion.

International Students

Advised on visa requirements, course load minimums, and academic progress in coordination with the International Student Office.

Students with Disabilities

Advisors work with the Office of Accessibility Services to ensure appropriate academic accommodations.

Resources

In addition to academic advisors, students have access to:

  • Academic Advising Center: General advising, major exploration, academic support
  • Career Services: Career planning, internships, job search assistance
  • Tutoring Center: Subject-specific tutoring
  • Counseling Services: Personal and mental health counseling
  • Financial Aid Office: Scholarships, financial aid, budgeting
