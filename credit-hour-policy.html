Credit Hour Policy

Federal Compliance & Academic Standards

Purpose

This policy defines the credit hour assignment for courses at Freedom College of Excellence in accordance with federal regulations (34 CFR §600.2) to ensure consistency in academic workload measurement and compliance with accreditation standards.

Definition of Credit Hour

A credit hour is an amount of work represented in intended learning outcomes and verified by evidence of student achievement. One credit hour represents:

  • Lecture/Instructional Time: One hour (50 minutes) of classroom or direct faculty instruction per week;
  • Out-of-Class Work: A minimum of two hours of out-of-class student work per week;
  • Total Weekly Commitment: Three hours of academic engagement per week;
  • Semester Duration: For a 15-week semester (or equivalent time for shorter terms).

Credit Hour Assignment by Course Type

1. Lecture Courses

Standard: 1 credit hour = 1 hour of instruction + 2 hours of out-of-class work per week for 15 weeks

Example: A 3-credit lecture course meets for 3 hours per week (e.g., Monday/Wednesday/Friday for 50 minutes each or Tuesday/Thursday for 75 minutes each) and requires 6 hours of out-of-class work per week.

2. Laboratory Courses

Standard: 1 credit hour = 2-3 hours of supervised laboratory work per week + 1 hour of out-of-class work

Example: A 1-credit lab meets for 2-3 hours per week and requires minimal out-of-class preparation.

3. Studio/Performance Courses

Standard: 1 credit hour = 2-3 hours of supervised studio practice or rehearsal per week + 1 hour of out-of-class work

Example: A 3-credit studio art course meets for 6-9 hours per week.

4. Internships/Practicums/Clinical Experiences

Standard: 1 credit hour = 3-4 hours of supervised field experience per week

Example: A 3-credit internship requires 9-12 hours of on-site work per week plus reflective assignments.

5. Online/Hybrid Courses

Standard: Credit hours are assigned based on equivalent workload to traditional courses, including synchronous meetings, asynchronous activities, and independent work.

Example: A 3-credit online course requires approximately 9 hours of total academic engagement per week (lectures, discussions, readings, assignments).

6. Independent Study/Research

Standard: 1 credit hour = 3 hours of independent work per week under faculty supervision

Example: A 3-credit independent study requires 9 hours of research, reading, or creative work per week plus regular meetings with faculty advisor.

Accelerated and Intensive Courses

Courses offered in accelerated formats (e.g., 8-week, 4-week, or 1-week intensive) must deliver the same total instructional and out-of-class hours as a traditional 15-week course.

Example: A 3-credit course offered in an 8-week format meets for 6 hours per week instead of 3 hours per week.

Course Development and Review

All new courses and programs must receive approval from the Curriculum Committee to ensure credit hour assignments comply with this policy. Factors considered include:

  • Course learning outcomes;
  • Instructional delivery method;
  • Expected student workload;
  • Alignment with federal credit hour definition.

Periodic Review

The College conducts periodic reviews of credit hour assignments to ensure compliance with federal regulations and to adjust for changes in pedagogy, technology, or accreditation standards.

Federal Compliance References

  • 34 CFR §600.2 – Definition of Credit Hour
  • 34 CFR §668.8 – Eligible Program
