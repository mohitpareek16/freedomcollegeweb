Attendance Policy

Class Participation Requirements

Purpose

Regular class attendance is essential for academic success. This policy establishes attendance expectations and outlines consequences for excessive absences.

General Attendance Expectations

Students are expected to attend all scheduled class meetings. Attendance may be required as part of course participation grades.

Faculty Attendance Policies

Individual instructors may establish specific attendance policies for their courses, which must be clearly outlined in the course syllabus. These policies may include:

  • Attendance as a percentage of the final grade;
  • Penalties for unexcused absences;
  • Maximum number of allowed absences before grade penalties or course failure;
  • Procedures for excused absences.

Excused Absences

The following absences are generally considered excused if proper documentation is provided:

  • Illness: Doctor's note required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days;
  • Family Emergency: Death or serious illness of an immediate family member (documentation required);
  • Religious Observances: Students must notify instructors at the beginning of the semester;
  • College-Sponsored Activities: Athletics, academic competitions, official college business (requires advance notice and documentation from sponsoring department);
  • Military Service: Active duty orders, reserve training (documentation required);
  • Court Appearances: Jury duty, subpoenas (documentation required).

Student Responsibilities

Students who miss class due to an excused absence must:

  • Notify the instructor as soon as possible (before class if advance notice is possible);
  • Provide documentation within one week of returning to class;
  • Arrange to make up missed work according to instructor guidelines;
  • Complete all assigned readings, homework, and projects.

Excessive Absences

Students who miss more than 20% of class meetings (excused or unexcused) may be subject to:

  • Automatic course failure (grade of F);
  • Administrative withdrawal from the course;
  • Loss of financial aid eligibility.

Example: In a course that meets 3 hours per week for 15 weeks (45 total hours), missing more than 9 hours may result in failure.

Federal Financial Aid Requirements

Students receiving federal financial aid must attend classes. Failure to attend may result in:

  • Return of Title IV funds;
  • Loss of financial aid eligibility;
  • Outstanding balance owed to the College.

Students who stop attending without officially withdrawing will receive a grade of F and may be required to repay financial aid.

Make-Up Work

Students with excused absences are entitled to make up missed work. Instructors will provide reasonable opportunities to complete assignments, quizzes, and exams. However, some activities (e.g., in-class presentations, lab work) may not be replicable.

Withdrawal for Non-Attendance

Instructors may initiate administrative withdrawal for students who miss the first two weeks of class without notification or who have excessive unexcused absences.

Appeals

Students who believe they have been unfairly penalized for absences may appeal through the Academic Grievance Policy.
