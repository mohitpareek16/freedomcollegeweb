Grading Policy

Academic Assessment Standards

Purpose

The Grading Policy establishes consistent standards for evaluating and reporting student academic performance at Freedom College of Excellence. This policy ensures transparency, fairness, and compliance with federal and accreditation standards.

Grading Scale

Freedom College of Excellence uses a letter grade system with the following scale:

Letter Grade Percentage Quality Points Description
A 93-100% 4.0 Excellent
A- 90-92% 3.7 Excellent
B+ 87-89% 3.3 Good
B 83-86% 3.0 Good
B- 80-82% 2.7 Good
C+ 77-79% 2.3 Satisfactory
C 73-76% 2.0 Satisfactory
C- 70-72% 1.7 Satisfactory
D+ 67-69% 1.3 Poor
D 63-66% 1.0 Poor
D- 60-62% 0.7 Poor
F 0-59% 0.0 Failing

Additional Grade Notations

  • W (Withdrawal): Student withdrew from course after Add/Drop Period; does not affect GPA
  • I (Incomplete): Temporary grade for extenuating circumstances; must be resolved within one semester
  • P (Pass): Passing grade in pass/fail courses; does not affect GPA
  • NP (No Pass): Failing grade in pass/fail courses; does not affect GPA but counts as attempted hours
  • AU (Audit): Student attended course without credit; does not affect GPA or count toward degree
  • IP (In Progress): For multi-term courses; replaced with final grade upon completion

Grade Point Average (GPA) Calculation

Formula: GPA = (Total Quality Points) ÷ (Total Credits Attempted)

Example:

  • Course 1: A (4.0) × 3 credits = 12.0 quality points
  • Course 2: B (3.0) × 4 credits = 12.0 quality points
  • Course 3: C+ (2.3) × 3 credits = 6.9 quality points
  • Total: 30.9 quality points ÷ 10 credits = 3.09 GPA

Incomplete Grades

An Incomplete (I) grade may be assigned when:

  • A student is unable to complete coursework due to extenuating circumstances beyond their control;
  • The student has completed at least 75% of course requirements with a passing grade;
  • The instructor and student agree on a completion plan.

Resolution: Incomplete grades must be resolved within one semester. If not resolved, the "I" automatically converts to an "F".

Repeated Courses

Students may repeat courses to improve their grade. The highest grade earned will be used in GPA calculation, but all attempts remain on the transcript.

Grade Appeals

Students who believe a grade was assigned in error may appeal following the Academic Grievance Policy.

Compliance References
