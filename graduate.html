Graduate Admissions Policy

Purpose

This policy outlines the principles, requirements, and procedures governing graduate admissions to Freedom College of Excellence. Our goal is to recruit, admit, and enroll a diverse and talented student body poised to contribute to and benefit from the College's unique academic environment at the graduate level.

Policy Statement

Freedom College of Excellence is committed to a holistic review process that evaluates each applicant's academic achievements, intellectual curiosity, professional experience, personal character, and potential for growth. We seek students who demonstrate a commitment to academic excellence, ethical leadership, and a spirit of inquiry, consistent with the College's mission.

General Admission Requirements

All applicants for graduate admission must meet the minimum requirements outlined below to be considered. For questions, please contact the Office of Graduate Admissions.

  1. Application Completion: Submit the Freedom College Graduate Application online, including the application fee or fee waiver request.
  2. Baccalaureate Degree: A bachelor's degree from a regionally accredited institution is required.
  3. Official Transcripts: Provide official transcripts from all post-secondary institutions attended.
  4. Letters of Recommendation: Submit three letters of recommendation from academic or professional references.
  5. Statement of Purpose: A written essay detailing your academic and professional goals and reasons for pursuing graduate study at Freedom College.
  6. Standardized Test Scores: Submission of GRE or other relevant test scores may be required depending on the specific graduate program.

Conditional Admission

In certain circumstances, an applicant who does not meet all the minimum requirements may be offered conditional admission. Conditions will be specified in the admission letter and must be met within the designated timeframe.

Transfer Credit Policy (Graduate Level)

A limited number of graduate credits may be transferred from another accredited institution, subject to program-specific policies and approval by the graduate program director and the registrar.

Application Review Process

Completed applications are reviewed by the graduate admissions committee for the respective program. The review is holistic, considering all components of the application to assess the applicant's potential for success.

Non-Degree-Seeking Graduate Students

Students wishing to take graduate courses without being formally admitted to a degree program may apply for non-degree-seeking status. Admission is subject to course availability and instructor permission.

Deferred Admission

Admitted students may request to defer their enrollment for up to one academic year. Deferral requests are reviewed on a case-by-case basis and require a non-refundable deposit to hold a place in the program.

Appeals

Applicants who are denied admission may appeal the decision in writing to the Office of Graduate Admissions. Appeals will be considered only if new and compelling information is provided that was not available during the initial review.

Recordkeeping

All application materials become the property of Freedom College of Excellence. The Office of Admissions is responsible for maintaining all graduate admission records in accordance with institutional policy and federal regulations.

Contact Admissions

Our admissions counselors are here to help guide you through the process.
