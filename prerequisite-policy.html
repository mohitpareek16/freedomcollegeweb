Prerequisite Policy

Course Enrollment Requirements

Purpose

Prerequisites ensure students have the necessary foundational knowledge and skills before enrolling in advanced courses. This policy outlines prerequisite requirements, enforcement, and waiver procedures.

What are Prerequisites?

Prerequisites are courses or conditions that must be completed before a student is permitted to enroll in a specific course. Prerequisites may include:

  • Course Prerequisites: Completion of specific courses with a minimum grade (e.g., "MAT 110 with a grade of C or better");
  • Test Score Requirements: Minimum scores on placement exams;
  • Class Standing: Sophomore, junior, or senior status;
  • Program Admission: Acceptance into a specific major or program;
  • Instructor Consent: Permission from the course instructor.

Finding Prerequisite Information

Prerequisites are listed in:

  • The Academic Catalog (course descriptions);
  • The online course schedule;
  • Degree audit reports;
  • Course syllabi.

Minimum Grades for Prerequisites

Unless otherwise specified, the following minimum grades are required:

Course Level Minimum Grade
100-200 Level Prerequisites C (2.0)
300-400 Level Prerequisites C (2.0)
500-600 Level (Graduate) Prerequisites B (3.0)
Major-Specific Prerequisites C+ (2.5) or as specified

Note: Some courses may require higher minimum grades (e.g., B or better). Always check the course description.

Co-requisites

Co-requisites are courses that must be taken concurrently (in the same semester). Students may enroll in both the course and its co-requisite simultaneously.

Example: CHE 101 (General Chemistry I) and CHE 101L (General Chemistry I Lab) are co-requisites.

Prerequisite Enforcement

The registration system automatically checks prerequisites at the time of enrollment. Students who do not meet prerequisites will be:

  • Blocked from registering for the course online;
  • Dropped from a course if prerequisite verification reveals non-compliance.

Responsibility: Students are responsible for ensuring they meet all prerequisites before enrolling in a course.

Prerequisite Overrides and Waivers

Students who believe they meet the prerequisite through alternative means (e.g., work experience, transfer credit not yet evaluated) may request an override:

Process

  1. Contact the course instructor or department chair to discuss qualifications;
  2. Provide documentation of equivalent knowledge or experience;
  3. If approved, the instructor or department chair submits a Prerequisite Override form to the Registrar;
  4. The Registrar grants a registration override.

Grounds for Override

  • Transfer credit for equivalent course (pending official evaluation);
  • Professional experience demonstrating equivalent competency;
  • Completion of a higher-level course in the same sequence;
  • Instructor determination that the student is prepared.

Denial of Override

Instructors and departments have the authority to deny prerequisite overrides. Students who disagree may appeal to the department chair or academic dean.

Concurrent Enrollment in Prerequisite and Subsequent Course

Students may not enroll in a course and its prerequisite in the same semester unless:

  • The courses are designated as co-requisites;
  • The instructor grants explicit permission;
  • The prerequisite is scheduled to end before the subsequent course begins (e.g., 8-week sessions).

Failing a Prerequisite

Students who fail a prerequisite course while enrolled in a subsequent course will:

  • Be required to retake the prerequisite;
  • May be removed from the subsequent course at the discretion of the instructor.

Students should consult with their advisor if they earn a grade lower than required in a prerequisite course.

Placement Exams

Some courses require placement exam scores as prerequisites:

  • Mathematics: Math placement test determines eligibility for MAT 110 (College Algebra) or higher;
  • Foreign Languages: Language placement determines appropriate course level;
  • English: Writing placement may be required for ENG 101.

Students may retake placement exams after a waiting period (typically 30 days).
