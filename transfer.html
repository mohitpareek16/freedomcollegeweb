Transfer Student Policy

Purpose

This policy outlines the requirements and procedures for students seeking to transfer to Freedom College of Excellence. Our aim is to provide a clear and equitable pathway for transfer students to join our academic community and continue their educational journey.

Policy Statement

Freedom College of Excellence welcomes the diverse perspectives and experiences that transfer students bring. We are committed to a comprehensive and holistic review of transfer applications, considering academic history, personal achievements, and potential for success within our rigorous academic programs.

Admission of Transfer Students

Applicants are considered transfer students if they have completed post-secondary coursework at a regionally accredited college or university following high school graduation. The following are required for consideration:

  1. Completed Application: Submission of the Common Application or the Freedom College Application for transfer students, along with the non-refundable application fee.
  2. Official College Transcripts: Official transcripts from all previously attended post-secondary institutions are required.
  3. Academic Standing: Applicants must be in good academic and disciplinary standing at their most recent institution.
  4. Minimum GPA: A minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5 on a 4.0 scale is typically required for consideration, though specific programs may have higher requirements.
  5. Official High School Transcript: Required for applicants with fewer than 24 transferable college credits.
  6. Personal Statement: An essay addressing the student's reasons for transferring and their academic goals at Freedom College.

Acceptance of Transfer Credits After Enrollment

Credit for courses completed at other regionally accredited institutions will be evaluated on a course-by-course basis. Credit is generally awarded for courses that are comparable to those offered at Freedom College and in which a grade of C or better was earned.

Impact on Financial Aid and Academic Standing

The number of accepted transfer credits may affect a student's eligibility for certain types of financial aid and their classification (e.g., sophomore, junior). Students should consult with the Office of Financial Aid for specific details.

Articulation Agreements

Freedom College of Excellence maintains articulation agreements with several community colleges to facilitate a seamless transfer process. Students transferring from partner institutions should consult the specific agreement details.

International Transfer Credits

Applicants with coursework from institutions outside the United States must provide an official course-by-course evaluation from a recognized credential evaluation service (e.g., WES, ECE).

Appeal of Transfer Credit Decisions

Students may appeal a transfer credit evaluation by submitting a written request to the Office of the Registrar. The appeal must include the course syllabus and any other relevant supporting documentation for review.

Recordkeeping

All application materials, including transcripts and evaluations, become the property of Freedom College of Excellence and will be maintained in the student's official academic record in accordance with institutional policies and FERPA regulations.
