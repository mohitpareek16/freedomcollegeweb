Transfer Credit Policy

Credit Evaluation & Acceptance

Purpose

The Transfer Credit Policy establishes guidelines for evaluating and accepting coursework completed at other regionally accredited institutions.

Eligibility for Transfer Credit

Transfer credit will be awarded for courses that meet the following criteria:

  • Institutional Accreditation: Completed at a regionally accredited college or university;
  • Grade Requirement: Minimum grade of C (2.0) for undergraduate courses; B (3.0) for graduate courses;
  • Course Equivalency: Comparable in content, level, and credit hours to courses offered at Freedom College;
  • Recency: Courses in technical or rapidly changing fields (e.g., computer science, healthcare) must have been completed within 10 years.

Maximum Transfer Credits

Program Level Maximum Transfer Credits
Associate Degree (60 credits) 45 credits (75%)
Bachelor's Degree (120 credits) 90 credits (75%)
Master's Degree (36 credits) 9 credits (25%)

Residency Requirement: Students must complete a minimum of 25% of their degree requirements in residence at Freedom College.

Types of Transfer Credit

1. College/University Coursework

Official transcripts must be submitted directly from the issuing institution. Courses are evaluated by the Registrar's Office in consultation with academic departments.

2. Advanced Placement (AP)

Students who earn a score of 3 or higher on AP exams may receive college credit. Credit awards vary by subject and score.

3. International Baccalaureate (IB)

Students who earn a score of 4 or higher on IB Higher Level (HL) exams may receive college credit.

4. CLEP (College-Level Examination Program)

Students who achieve the minimum recommended score on CLEP exams (typically 50 or higher) may receive credit.

5. Military Training

Credit may be awarded for military training and experience as recommended by the American Council on Education (ACE). Students must submit Joint Services Transcript (JST) or equivalent.

6. International Coursework

Coursework completed at institutions outside the United States must be evaluated by a NACES-approved credential evaluation service (e.g., WES, ECE). Students are responsible for evaluation costs.

Transfer Credit Evaluation Process

  1. Submit Official Transcripts: Request transcripts from all previously attended institutions to be sent directly to the Office of Admissions;
  2. Preliminary Evaluation: Unofficial evaluation provided upon admission;
  3. Official Evaluation: Final transfer credit evaluation completed after receipt of official transcripts;
  4. Course Equivalency: Courses are matched to Freedom College equivalents or listed as elective credit;
  5. Notification: Students receive a Transfer Credit Summary listing accepted courses and applied credits.

Transfer Credit Appeal

Students who disagree with transfer credit decisions may submit a written appeal to the Registrar within 30 days of notification. Appeals must include:

  • Course syllabus;
  • Course description from catalog;
  • Rationale for why the course should be accepted.

Effect on GPA

Transfer credits count toward degree completion but do not affect the student's Freedom College GPA. Only courses completed at Freedom College are included in GPA calculations.

Consortium Agreements

Students may enroll in courses at consortium partner institutions with pre-approval from their academic advisor and the Registrar. Credits earned through consortium agreements transfer automatically.
