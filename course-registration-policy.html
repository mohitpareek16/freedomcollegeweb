Course Registration Policy

Enrollment Procedures & Deadlines

Purpose

The Course Registration Policy outlines procedures, timelines, and requirements for enrolling in courses at Freedom College of Excellence.

Registration Eligibility

To be eligible for registration, students must:

  • Be admitted to the College;
  • Have no registration holds (financial, academic, advising, health);
  • Meet prerequisites for the courses;
  • Be in good academic standing (unless on academic probation with approval).

Registration Periods

Priority Registration

Registration opens in waves based on student classification and earned credits:

Priority Group Registration Window
Graduate Students & Seniors (90+ credits) Week 1
Juniors (60-89 credits) Week 2
Sophomores (30-59 credits) Week 3
Freshmen & New Transfer Students (0-29 credits) Week 4

Note: Specific registration dates are published by the Registrar's Office 6-8 weeks before the start of registration.

Open Registration

After priority registration closes, open registration continues until the end of the Add/Drop period (typically the first week of classes).

How to Register

Online Registration (Preferred):

  1. Log in to the student portal;
  2. Navigate to "Registration" or "Add/Drop Classes";
  3. Search for courses by subject, number, or instructor;
  4. Select desired sections and add to schedule;
  5. Submit registration.

In-Person/Email Registration: Contact the Registrar's Office for assistance if unable to register online.

Course Load Limits

Student Type Minimum Standard Maximum
Undergraduate (Full-Time) 12 credits 15-16 credits 18 credits
Undergraduate (Part-Time) 1 credit 6-9 credits 11 credits
Graduate (Full-Time) 9 credits 9-12 credits 12 credits
Graduate (Part-Time) 1 credit 3-6 credits 8 credits

Overload: Students wishing to exceed the maximum course load must petition the Registrar with a written request and academic advisor approval. Typically requires a GPA of 3.0 or higher.

Registration Holds

Common holds that prevent registration:

  • Financial Hold: Outstanding balance owed to the College;
  • Advising Hold: Required advising appointment not completed;
  • Immunization Hold: Missing required health records;
  • Academic Hold: On academic suspension or missing documents.

Clearing Holds: Contact the appropriate office (Bursar, Advising Center, Health Services, Registrar) to resolve holds before registration.

Waitlists

When a course is full, students may add themselves to a waitlist:

  • Waitlist positions are granted on a first-come, first-served basis;
  • Students are notified via email if a seat becomes available;
  • Students have 24 hours to accept the seat or forfeit it to the next student on the waitlist;
  • Waitlists are purged at the end of the Add/Drop period.

Closed Courses and Permission Numbers

Students seeking entry to a closed course must:

  1. Contact the course instructor to request permission;
  2. If granted, the instructor provides a permission/authorization number;
  3. Student enters the permission number during online registration.

Note: Instructors are not obligated to grant permission for overenrollment.

Repeating Courses

Students may repeat courses to improve grades (see Grade Replacement Policy). When repeating a course:

  • The highest grade is used in GPA calculations (if permitted by policy);
  • All attempts appear on the transcript;
  • Credit is earned only once;
  • Financial aid may not cover repeated courses beyond specific limits.

Late Registration

Late registration is permitted through the end of the Add/Drop period (typically the first week of classes) with a $50 late registration fee.

Students who register after the first day of classes are responsible for missed coursework.

Auditing Courses

Students may audit courses (attend without earning credit) with instructor permission. Auditors:

  • Pay full tuition;
  • Are not required to complete assignments or exams;
  • Receive a grade of "AU" (Audit) on their transcript;
  • Cannot convert an audit to credit or vice versa after the Add/Drop period.

Pass/Fail Option

Undergraduate students may take elective courses on a pass/fail basis (subject to restrictions). See the Pass/Fail Policy for details.
