Major & Minor Requirements

Declaring & Completing Your Program

Major Requirements

What is a Major?

A major is a student's primary field of study, consisting of focused coursework designed to develop expertise in a specific discipline.

Credit Requirements by Degree Level

Degree Level Major Credits Minimum GPA
Associate Degree 24-30 credits 2.0
Bachelor's Degree 36-48 credits 2.5
Master's Degree 30-36 credits (entire program) 3.0

Declaring a Major

Undergraduate Students:

  • Freshmen: May enter with an intended major but are not required to formally declare until completing 30 credits;
  • Sophomores: Must declare a major before completing 60 credits;
  • Transfer Students: Should declare upon admission or within the first semester of enrollment.

Process: Submit a Declaration of Major form to the Registrar's Office with the signature of the department advisor.

Changing Your Major

Students may change their major by:

  1. Meeting with the current and prospective academic advisors;
  2. Submitting a Change of Major form to the Registrar's Office;
  3. Understanding how the change affects degree progress, financial aid, and graduation timeline.

Note: Courses from the former major may count as electives if applicable.

Double Majors

Students may pursue two majors simultaneously. Requirements:

  • Complete all requirements for both majors;
  • Achieve minimum GPA (2.5) in each major;
  • At least 12 credits must be unique to each major (no double-counting);
  • Typically requires 15-30 additional credits beyond a single major.

Designation: Both majors appear on the transcript and diploma.

Upper-Level Coursework

Bachelor's degree students must complete at least 50% of major credits at the 300-400 level (upper division).

Capstone Requirement

Many majors require a capstone experience, which may include:

  • Senior seminar or capstone course;
  • Research thesis or project;
  • Internship or practicum;
  • Comprehensive examination.

Check with your department for specific requirements.

Minor Requirements

What is a Minor?

A minor is a secondary area of study, allowing students to develop knowledge in a field outside their major.

Credit Requirements

Minor Type Credit Hours Minimum GPA
Standard Minor 18-21 credits 2.0
Interdisciplinary Minor 18-24 credits 2.0

Note: Minors are optional for most programs but may be required for specific majors (e.g., Education majors may require a content area minor).

Declaring a Minor

Students may declare a minor at any time by:

  1. Meeting with an advisor in the minor department;
  2. Submitting a Declaration of Minor form to the Registrar's Office;
  3. Ensuring no more than 6 credits overlap with major requirements.

Requirements

  • Unique Courses: At least 12 credits in the minor must not count toward the major;
  • Upper-Level Coursework: At least 9 credits must be at the 300-400 level;
  • Residency: Minimum 9 credits of the minor completed at Freedom College.

Multiple Minors

Students may complete more than one minor, subject to advisor approval and degree audit verification that all requirements are met.

Concentration vs. Minor

Concentration: A specialization within a major (e.g., Marketing concentration within Business Administration major). Appears on transcript but not on diploma.

Minor: Secondary field of study distinct from the major. Appears on both transcript and diploma.
