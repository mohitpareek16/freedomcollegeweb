Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)

Maintaining Financial Aid Eligibility

Purpose

The Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) Policy establishes academic standards that students must meet to remain eligible for federal, state, and institutional financial aid at Freedom College of Excellence.

This policy ensures compliance with Title IV of the Higher Education Act (34 CFR Â§668.34) and supports student success by promoting timely degree completion and academic achievement.

Scope

This policy applies to all students enrolled in degree or certificate programs who receive or apply for federal student aid (Title IV), state aid, or institutional scholarships and grants.

SAP is evaluated for all students regardless of whether they previously received financial aid.

Policy Statement

To maintain financial aid eligibility, students must demonstrate Satisfactory Academic Progress by meeting qualitative (GPA), quantitative (completion rate), and maximum timeframe requirements.

SAP is evaluated at the end of each payment period (semester or term). Students who fail to meet SAP standards will be placed on Financial Aid Warning, Financial Aid Suspension, or may regain eligibility through an approved SAP Appeal.

SAP Standards

Students must meet all three of the following standards:

1. Qualitative Standard: Minimum Cumulative GPA

Program Level Minimum Cumulative GPA
Undergraduate 2.0
Graduate 3.0

2. Quantitative Standard: Completion Rate (Pace)

Students must successfully complete at least 67% of all attempted credit hours.

Includes:

  • All courses attempted at Freedom College of Excellence;
  • Transfer credits accepted toward the student's program;
  • Withdrawals (W), Incompletes (I), and Failing grades (F).

Excludes: Audited courses, courses dropped during the Add/Drop Period.

3. Maximum Timeframe

Students must complete their degree or certificate within 150% of the published program length.

Program Type Published Length Maximum Timeframe (150%)
Associate Degree 60 credits 90 credits
Bachelor's Degree 120 credits 180 credits
Master's Degree 36 credits 54 credits

SAP Evaluation

SAP is reviewed at the end of each payment period (semester or term) by the Financial Aid Office.

  • All coursework at Freedom College of Excellence is included in the calculation;
  • Transfer credits accepted toward the student's program are included;
  • Repeated courses count as both attempted and earned (only once if passed);
  • Incompletes (I) and Withdrawals (W) count as attempted but not earned.

SAP Status Categories

1. Meeting SAP

Students who meet all three SAP standards remain eligible for financial aid.

2. Financial Aid Warning

Students who fail to meet SAP for the first time are automatically placed on Financial Aid Warning for one payment period.

  • Financial aid eligibility continues during the Warning period;
  • No appeal is required;
  • Students must meet all SAP standards by the end of the Warning period to remain eligible.

3. Financial Aid Suspension

Students who fail to meet SAP after the Warning period are placed on Financial Aid Suspension and lose eligibility for all Title IV, state, and institutional aid.

Students may regain eligibility by:

  • Successfully appealing their suspension; or
  • Attending without aid until SAP standards are met.

SAP Appeal Process

Students on Financial Aid Suspension may submit a written appeal if extenuating circumstances prevented them from meeting SAP.

Grounds for Appeal:

  • Serious illness or injury (student or immediate family member);
  • Death of an immediate family member;
  • Military deployment or service obligations;
  • Other documented emergency or unusual circumstances.

Appeal Requirements:

  1. Submit a written appeal to the Financial Aid Office within 10 business days of suspension notice;
  2. Include a detailed explanation of the extenuating circumstances;
  3. Provide supporting documentation (e.g., medical records, death certificate, deployment orders);
  4. Submit an Academic Plan (developed with an Academic Advisor) outlining how the student will meet SAP by a specific deadline.

Appeal Outcomes:

  • Approved: Student is placed on Financial Aid Probation and must follow the Academic Plan;
  • Denied: Student remains on suspension; may reapply after completing coursework without aid to meet SAP.

Financial Aid Probation

Students whose appeals are approved are placed on Financial Aid Probation for one payment period.

  • Financial aid is reinstated;
  • Students must meet the conditions of their Academic Plan and/or meet all SAP standards by the end of the Probation period;
  • Failure to comply results in immediate suspension.

Regaining Eligibility

Students who lose aid due to SAP may regain eligibility by:

  • Successfully completing enough credits (without aid) to meet all three SAP standards; or
  • Successfully appealing their suspension and meeting the terms of their Academic Plan.

Notification

Students will be notified of their SAP status via:

  • Email to the official College email address;
  • Written notice sent by postal mail; and
  • Notification in the student portal.
