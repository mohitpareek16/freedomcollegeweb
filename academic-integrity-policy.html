Academic Integrity Policy

Upholding Honesty & Ethical Standards

Purpose

Freedom College of Excellence is committed to maintaining the highest standards of academic integrity. This policy defines academic dishonesty, outlines consequences for violations, and establishes procedures for reporting and adjudicating alleged violations.

Fundamental Principles

All members of the College community are expected to:

  • Produce original work and give proper credit to the intellectual contributions of others;
  • Refrain from obtaining unfair academic advantage;
  • Submit only their own work for academic evaluation;
  • Report suspected violations of academic integrity.

Forms of Academic Dishonesty

1. Plagiarism

Using another person's ideas, words, data, or creative work without proper attribution. This includes:

  • Copying text from sources without quotation marks or citations;
  • Paraphrasing others' work without proper citation;
  • Submitting work created by someone else (including AI-generated content without disclosure);
  • Self-plagiarism (submitting the same work for multiple courses without permission).

2. Cheating

Using unauthorized materials, information, or assistance in academic work, including:

  • Using unauthorized notes, devices, or materials during exams;
  • Copying answers from another student;
  • Receiving unauthorized assistance on assignments;
  • Collaborating on individual assignments when prohibited.

3. Fabrication

Falsifying or inventing data, citations, or information, including:

  • Creating false data or research results;
  • Citing sources that do not exist;
  • Altering graded work and resubmitting for re-grading.

4. Facilitation

Helping another student commit academic dishonesty, including:

  • Allowing another student to copy your work;
  • Taking an exam or completing an assignment for another student;
  • Providing unauthorized assistance.

5. Unauthorized Collaboration

Working with others on assignments designated as individual work without instructor permission.

Reporting Violations

Faculty members who suspect a violation must:

  1. Document the suspected violation with evidence;
  2. Meet with the student to discuss the concern;
  3. Report the incident to the Academic Integrity Officer within 5 business days.

Investigation and Adjudication

Upon receiving a report, the Academic Integrity Officer will:

  1. Notify the student in writing of the alleged violation;
  2. Conduct an investigation, which may include interviewing the student, faculty member, and witnesses;
  3. Make a determination based on a preponderance of evidence;
  4. Impose sanctions if a violation is found.

Sanctions

Sanctions for academic integrity violations may include:

First Offense (Minor Violations):

  • Warning and required attendance at academic integrity workshop;
  • Grade penalty on the assignment (e.g., zero on the assignment);
  • Grade penalty in the course (e.g., reduction of one letter grade).

Second Offense or Serious First Offense:

  • Failure in the course (grade of F);
  • Academic probation;
  • Notation on transcript ("Academic Integrity Violation").

Third Offense or Severe Violations:

  • Suspension for one or more semesters;
  • Expulsion from the College;
  • Revocation of degree (if violation discovered after graduation).

Appeals

Students may appeal a finding of academic dishonesty or the assigned sanction by submitting a written appeal to the Academic Appeals Committee within 10 business days of notification. Appeals must be based on:

  • Procedural error that affected the outcome;
  • New evidence not available during the original investigation;
  • Sanction disproportionate to the violation.

Records

Records of academic integrity violations are maintained by the Academic Integrity Officer and may be considered in determining sanctions for subsequent violations. Records are retained for 7 years after the student's last enrollment.

Educational Resources

The College provides resources to help students understand and uphold academic integrity, including:

  • Academic Integrity Workshop (required for all first-year students);
  • Writing Center support for proper citation and paraphrasing;
  • Online tutorials on avoiding plagiarism.
