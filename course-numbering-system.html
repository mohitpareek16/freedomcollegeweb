Course Numbering System

Understanding Course Codes

Overview

The course numbering system at Freedom College of Excellence provides a standardized method for identifying courses by discipline, level, and sequence. Understanding this system helps students plan their academic path and select appropriate courses.

Course Code Structure

Format: PREFIX ### (Letter)

Example: ENG 101
ENG = Subject/Department Prefix
101 = Course Level and Sequence
(Optional Letter) = Lab or Section Designator

Subject Prefixes

The three- or four-letter prefix indicates the academic discipline or department. Common prefixes include:

Prefix Department
ENG English
MAT Mathematics
BIO Biology
CHE Chemistry
PSY Psychology
HIS History
BUS Business
COM Communication

See the Academic Catalog for a complete list of subject prefixes.

Course Numbering Levels

Number Range Level Description
001-099 Developmental Non-credit remedial courses; do not count toward degree
100-199 Freshman/Introductory Lower-division courses; typically no prerequisites
200-299 Sophomore/Intermediate Lower-division courses; may have prerequisites
300-399 Junior/Advanced Upper-division courses; usually require prerequisites
400-499 Senior/Specialized Upper-division courses; significant prerequisites
500-599 Graduate Level I Master's level courses; graduate admission required
600-699 Graduate Level II Advanced master's coursework, thesis, capstone

Specific Number Meanings

X01 - X02: Course Sequences

Courses ending in 01 and 02 typically indicate a two-semester sequence:

  • ENG 101 - English Composition I
  • ENG 102 - English Composition II

X90-X99: Special Topics & Capstone

  • X90-X94: Special Topics courses (content varies by semester)
  • X95: Independent Study
  • X96: Internship/Practicum
  • X98: Senior Seminar/Capstone
  • X99: Thesis or Research Project

Letter Suffixes

  • L: Laboratory component (e.g., CHE 101L - Chemistry Lab)
  • H: Honors section
  • W: Writing Intensive
  • D: Diversity/Global Studies designation

Lower Division vs. Upper Division

Lower Division: 100-200 level courses. Foundational knowledge, typically taken in first two years.

Upper Division: 300-400 level courses. Advanced study within a discipline, typically taken in final two years. Bachelor's degrees require a minimum of 45 upper-division credits.

Credit Hours

Credit hours are indicated in parentheses in the course catalog:

  • ENG 101 (3) - 3 credit hours
  • BIO 101 (4) - 4 credit hours (typically includes lab)

Course Repeats and Variations

Courses with the same prefix and number but different titles may indicate:

  • Variable Content: Special Topics courses that change each semester;
  • Repeatable Courses: Some courses (e.g., applied music, studio art) may be repeated for credit.

Check the course description for repeatability status.

  • 301: Junior-level (300 series), upper-division
  • H: Honors section
  • (3): 3 credit hours
