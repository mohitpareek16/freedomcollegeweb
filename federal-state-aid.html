Federal & State Aid Programs

Making Education Accessible

Overview

Freedom College of Excellence participates in federal and state financial aid programs to help students and families afford the cost of higher education. This page provides an overview of available federal Title IV aid programs, state grant programs, and the process for applying and maintaining eligibility.

Federal Student Aid (Title IV Programs)

Freedom College of Excellence is an eligible institution under Title IV of the Higher Education Act and participates in the following federal student aid programs administered by the U.S. Department of Education.

Eligibility Requirements

To receive federal student aid, students must:

  • Be a U.S. citizen or eligible non-citizen;
  • Have a valid Social Security Number;
  • Be enrolled or accepted for enrollment in an eligible degree or certificate program;
  • Maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) as defined by the College's SAP Policy;
  • Not be in default on any federal student loan or owe a refund on a federal grant;
  • Register with Selective Service (if required);
  • Have a high school diploma, GED, or approved homeschool completion.

Federal Grant Programs

1. Federal Pell Grant

Award Amount: Up to $7,395 per year (2024-2025)

Eligibility:

  • Undergraduate students only;
  • Based on financial need as determined by the FAFSA;
  • Enrollment status affects award amount (full-time, three-quarter-time, half-time, less than half-time);
  • Lifetime Eligibility Used (LEU) cannot exceed 600% (equivalent to 6 full-time years).

How to Apply: Complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) at studentaid.gov.

Disbursement: Awarded per semester; credited directly to the student's account to cover tuition, fees, and other charges. Any remaining funds are refunded to the student.

2. Federal Supplemental Educational Opportunity Grant (FSEOG)

Award Amount: $100 - $4,000 per year (based on availability)

Eligibility:

  • Undergraduate students with exceptional financial need;
  • Priority given to Pell Grant recipients;
  • Campus-based program; funds are limited and awarded on a first-come, first-served basis.

How to Apply: Complete the FAFSA as early as possible (priority deadline: March 1).

3. Iraq and Afghanistan Service Grant

Award Amount: Equal to the maximum Pell Grant amount

Eligibility:

  • Students whose parent or guardian died as a result of military service in Iraq or Afghanistan after September 11, 2001;
  • Student must be under 24 years old or enrolled at least part-time at the time of the parent's death;
  • Not eligible for a Pell Grant based on Expected Family Contribution (EFC).

How to Apply: Complete the FAFSA.

Federal Work-Study (FWS)

Award Amount: Varies (based on financial need and available funding)

Description: Federal Work-Study provides part-time employment opportunities for students with financial need, allowing them to earn money to help cover educational expenses.

Eligibility:

  • Undergraduate and graduate students with demonstrated financial need;
  • Campus-based program with limited funding;
  • Students must apply for and secure a work-study position through the College's Career Services Office.

Earnings:

  • Students are paid at least minimum wage (or higher, depending on the position);
  • Paychecks are issued bi-weekly; earnings are not automatically applied to tuition;
  • Students may work up to 20 hours per week during the academic term and up to 40 hours during breaks.

Federal Student Loans

1. Direct Subsidized Loan (Undergraduate Only)

Loan Amount:

  • Freshmen: Up to $3,500
  • Sophomores: Up to $4,500
  • Juniors/Seniors: Up to $5,500

Features:

  • Based on financial need;
  • The U.S. Department of Education pays the interest while the student is enrolled at least half-time, during the grace period, and during authorized deferment periods;

2. Direct Unsubsidized Loan

Loan Amount:

  • Undergraduates: $2,000 - $7,500 per year (in addition to Subsidized Loan limits, if eligible)
  • Graduate Students: Up to $20,500 per year

Features:

  • Not based on financial need;
  • Interest accrues from the time of disbursement; students may choose to pay interest while in school or allow it to capitalize;
  • Interest rate: 5.50% (undergraduate), 7.05% (graduate) â€“ fixed rates for loans disbursed July 1, 2023 â€“ June 30, 2024.

3. Direct PLUS Loan (Parent and Graduate)

Parent PLUS Loan (for parents of dependent undergraduate students):

  • Loan Amount: Up to the cost of attendance minus other financial aid received;
  • Features: Credit check required; cosigner not permitted; interest rate: 8.05% (fixed).

Graduate PLUS Loan (for graduate students):

  • Loan Amount: Up to the cost of attendance minus other financial aid received;
  • Features: Credit check required; interest rate: 8.05% (fixed).

Loan Repayment and Counseling

All federal student loan borrowers are required to complete:

  • Entrance Counseling: Before receiving the first loan disbursement (completed online at studentaid.gov);
  • Exit Counseling: Before graduating, withdrawing, or dropping below half-time enrollment.

Repayment:

  • Repayment begins six months after graduation, withdrawal, or dropping below half-time status;
  • Multiple repayment plans are available, including income-driven repayment options;
  • For more information, visit studentaid.gov or contact your loan servicer.

State Financial Aid Programs

Freedom College of Excellence partners with state agencies to provide additional grant and scholarship opportunities for eligible students. Availability and eligibility vary by state.

State Grant Example Programs:

  • State Need-Based Grant: For residents demonstrating financial need; requires FAFSA completion;
  • State Merit Scholarship: For high-achieving students based on GPA and test scores;
  • State-Specific Workforce Grants: For students pursuing careers in high-demand fields (e.g., nursing, teaching, technology).

How to Apply: Complete the FAFSA and any required state-specific applications. Contact the Financial Aid Office for state-specific deadlines and requirements.

How to Apply for Federal and State Aid

Step 1: Complete the FAFSA

Visit studentaid.gov and complete the Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA).

Freedom College of Excellence Federal School Code: 123456

Priority Deadline: March 1 (for Fall term)

Step 2: Review Your Student Aid Report (SAR)

After submitting the FAFSA, you will receive a Student Aid Report summarizing your information and Expected Family Contribution (EFC). Review for accuracy and make corrections if needed.

Step 3: Receive Your Financial Aid Award Letter

The Financial Aid Office will send an official award letter detailing your eligibility for grants, loans, and work-study. Review your award carefully.

Step 4: Accept or Decline Awards

Log in to your student portal to accept, decline, or adjust loan amounts. You are not required to accept all offered aid.

Step 5: Complete Required Documents

  • Entrance Counseling (for first-time loan borrowers);
  • Master Promissory Note (MPN) for loans;
  • Verification documents (if selected by the Department of Education).

Verification Process

Some students are selected for verification by the U.S. Department of Education. If selected, you must submit:

  • IRS Tax Return Transcript or W-2 forms;
  • Verification Worksheet (provided by the Financial Aid Office);
  • Proof of household size and number in college (if applicable).

Financial aid will not disburse until verification is complete. Submit documents as soon as possible to avoid delays.

Maintaining Eligibility

To continue receiving federal and state aid, students must:

  • Maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) â€“ see SAP Policy;
  • Enroll at least half-time (6 credits per semester) for most aid programs;
  • Reapply for aid annually by completing the FAFSA;
  • Not be in default on federal loans or owe a refund on federal grants;
  • Remain in good academic and disciplinary standing.
