Scholarships & Merit-Based Awards

Recognizing Academic Excellence

Purpose

Freedom College of Excellence is committed to recognizing and supporting exceptional students through institutional scholarships and merit-based awards. These awards are designed to reward academic achievement, leadership, community involvement, and specialized talents.

This policy outlines eligibility criteria, application procedures, award amounts, renewal requirements, and administrative guidelines for all institutional scholarships and merit-based awards.

Scope

This policy applies to all prospective and currently enrolled students seeking or receiving institutional scholarships or merit-based awards at Freedom College of Excellence, including undergraduate, graduate, and certificate programs.

Policy Statement

Freedom College of Excellence offers competitive merit-based scholarships to recognize academic excellence, leadership potential, and student achievements. Scholarships are awarded based on objective criteria and are subject to annual renewal contingent upon the student maintaining satisfactory academic progress and meeting program-specific requirements.

All scholarship decisions are made by the Scholarship Committee in accordance with established guidelines and available institutional funding.

Types of Scholarships and Awards

1. Presidential Scholarship

Award Amount: Full tuition (up to $20,000 per year)

Eligibility:

  • Incoming freshmen with a minimum 3.8 GPA and 1400 SAT or 30 ACT;
  • Demonstrated leadership and community service;
  • Full-time enrollment required.

Renewal Requirements: Maintain a cumulative 3.5 GPA and full-time enrollment.

2. Dean's Scholarship

Award Amount: $15,000 per year

Eligibility:

  • Incoming freshmen with a minimum 3.5 GPA and 1300 SAT or 27 ACT;
  • Strong academic record and extracurricular involvement;
  • Full-time enrollment required.

Renewal Requirements: Maintain a cumulative 3.25 GPA and full-time enrollment.

3. Academic Excellence Scholarship

Award Amount: $10,000 per year

Eligibility:

  • Incoming freshmen with a minimum 3.3 GPA and 1200 SAT or 24 ACT;
  • Evidence of academic achievement;
  • Full-time enrollment required.

Renewal Requirements: Maintain a cumulative 3.0 GPA and full-time enrollment.

4. Transfer Student Scholarship

Award Amount: $5,000 - $12,000 per year

Eligibility:

  • Transfer students with a minimum 3.0 college GPA;
  • At least 24 transferable credit hours;
  • Full-time enrollment required.

Renewal Requirements: Maintain a cumulative 3.0 GPA and full-time enrollment.

5. Graduate Merit Scholarship

Award Amount: $3,000 - $8,000 per year

Eligibility:

  • Admitted graduate students with a minimum 3.5 undergraduate GPA;
  • Strong academic references and statement of purpose;
  • Enrollment in a minimum of 6 credit hours per term.

Renewal Requirements: Maintain a cumulative 3.5 graduate GPA.

6. Diversity and Inclusion Scholarship

Award Amount: $7,500 per year

Eligibility:

  • Students who demonstrate a commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion;
  • Minimum 3.0 GPA;
  • Essay requirement on diversity and inclusion topics.

Renewal Requirements: Maintain a cumulative 3.0 GPA and continued engagement in diversity initiatives.

7. STEM Leadership Award

Award Amount: $6,000 per year

Eligibility:

  • Students majoring in Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics;
  • Minimum 3.2 GPA;
  • Evidence of STEM-related extracurricular activities or research.

Renewal Requirements: Maintain a cumulative 3.0 GPA in STEM coursework and remain in a STEM major.

8. Community Service Award

Award Amount: $4,000 per year

Eligibility:

  • Students with demonstrated commitment to community service (minimum 100 service hours);
  • Minimum 3.0 GPA;
  • Letters of recommendation from community organizations.

Renewal Requirements: Complete at least 50 hours of community service per year and maintain a 3.0 GPA.

Application Process

For Incoming Freshmen:

  1. Submit a completed application for admission by the priority deadline (December 1 for Fall, October 1 for Spring);
  2. All admitted students are automatically considered for merit scholarships based on their application materials;
  3. No separate scholarship application is required for Presidential, Dean's, or Academic Excellence Scholarships.

For Transfer Students:

  1. Submit a completed transfer application by the priority deadline;
  2. Official transcripts from all previously attended institutions must be received;
  3. Automatic consideration for Transfer Student Scholarship.

For Graduate Students:

  1. Submit a completed graduate application including official transcripts, letters of recommendation, and statement of purpose;
  2. Graduate Merit Scholarships are awarded by individual graduate programs based on academic qualifications.

For Specialized Awards (Diversity, STEM, Community Service):

  1. Submit a separate scholarship application available through the Financial Aid Office or student portal;
  2. Include required essays, letters of recommendation, and documentation of eligibility criteria;
  3. Application deadline: March 1 (for Fall term), October 1 (for Spring term).

Award Notification

Scholarship recipients will be notified via:

  • Official award letter sent by email and postal mail;
  • Award details posted in the student portal;
  • Financial aid package update.

Notification timeline:

  • Freshmen: Within 2 weeks of admission decision;
  • Transfer Students: Within 3 weeks of admission decision;
  • Graduate Students: Within 4 weeks of admission decision;
  • Specialized Awards: By April 15 (Fall) or November 15 (Spring).

Renewal and Continuation

Most institutional scholarships are renewable for up to four years (eight semesters) for undergraduates and two years (four semesters) for graduate students, provided the following conditions are met:

General Renewal Requirements:

  • Maintain the minimum cumulative GPA specified for each scholarship;
  • Enroll full-time (12+ credits for undergraduates; 6+ credits for graduate students) unless approved for part-time status;
  • Maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) as defined by the College's SAP Policy;
  • Remain in good academic and disciplinary standing;
  • Complete any program-specific requirements (e.g., community service hours, research participation).

Probation and Loss of Scholarship:

  • Students who fall below the required GPA will be placed on Scholarship Probation for one semester;
  • During probation, the scholarship is retained, but the student must meet the GPA requirement by the end of the probationary term;
  • Failure to meet requirements after probation results in permanent loss of the scholarship;
  • Scholarships lost due to academic performance cannot be reinstated.

Stacking and Combining Awards

Students may receive multiple institutional scholarships, but total scholarship and grant aid cannot exceed the cost of tuition and fees. If combined awards exceed tuition, the excess will be applied to room, board, and other educational expenses.

Institutional scholarships may be combined with federal, state, and external aid, subject to federal and state regulations.

Impact on Financial Aid

Institutional scholarships reduce the student's financial need and may impact need-based aid eligibility, including federal grants and work-study. The Financial Aid Office will recalculate the student's financial aid package upon scholarship award.

Appeals and Special Circumstances

Students who experience extenuating circumstances (e.g., serious illness, family emergency) that impact their ability to meet renewal requirements may submit a written appeal to the Scholarship Committee.

Appeal Requirements:

  1. Submit a formal written appeal within 15 business days of notification of scholarship loss or probation;
  2. Include documentation of extenuating circumstances;
  3. Provide an academic plan developed with an Academic Advisor;
  4. Appeals are reviewed on a case-by-case basis; decisions are final.

Compliance and Administrative Guidelines

  • All scholarships are subject to availability of institutional funds;
  • The College reserves the right to modify scholarship amounts, criteria, and renewal requirements with appropriate notice;
  • Scholarships are awarded on a competitive basis; meeting minimum criteria does not guarantee an award;
  • Scholarship funds are applied directly to the student's account and cannot be disbursed as cash.
