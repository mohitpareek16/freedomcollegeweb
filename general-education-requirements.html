General Education Requirements

Core Curriculum for All Undergraduates

Purpose

The General Education Core Curriculum provides all undergraduate students with a broad foundation in communication, critical thinking, quantitative reasoning, scientific inquiry, cultural awareness, and ethical decision-making.

Total General Education Credits

  • Associate Degree: 18-24 credits
  • Bachelor's Degree: 36-42 credits

Core Curriculum Areas

1. Communication (6-9 credits)

Required Courses:

  • ENG 101 - English Composition I (3 credits)
  • ENG 102 - English Composition II (3 credits)
  • COM 101 - Public Speaking (3 credits) [Bachelor's only]

Learning Outcomes: Develop effective written and oral communication skills, critical reading, research methodologies, and persuasive argument construction.

2. Mathematics & Quantitative Reasoning (3-6 credits)

Choose from:

  • MAT 110 - College Algebra (3 credits)
  • MAT 120 - Statistics (3 credits)
  • MAT 130 - Calculus I (4 credits)
  • MAT 140 - Finite Mathematics (3 credits)

Note: Bachelor's degree students pursuing STEM majors may require additional mathematics courses beyond the core.

Learning Outcomes: Apply mathematical concepts to solve problems, interpret quantitative data, and use logical reasoning.

3. Natural Sciences (6-8 credits)

Requirements: Two courses from different disciplines, at least one with a laboratory component.

Choose from:

  • BIO 101 - General Biology I (4 credits, with lab)
  • CHE 101 - General Chemistry I (4 credits, with lab)
  • PHY 101 - General Physics I (4 credits, with lab)
  • ENV 101 - Environmental Science (3 credits)
  • AST 101 - Introduction to Astronomy (3 credits)

Learning Outcomes: Understand scientific methods, analyze empirical evidence, and recognize the role of science in society.

4. Social & Behavioral Sciences (6-9 credits)

Requirements: Two or three courses from at least two different disciplines.

Choose from:

  • PSY 101 - Introduction to Psychology (3 credits)
  • SOC 101 - Introduction to Sociology (3 credits)
  • ECO 201 - Macroeconomics (3 credits)
  • ECO 202 - Microeconomics (3 credits)
  • POL 101 - American Government (3 credits)
  • ANT 101 - Cultural Anthropology (3 credits)

Learning Outcomes: Analyze human behavior, social structures, and cultural diversity; understand historical and contemporary social issues.

5. Humanities & Fine Arts (6-9 credits)

Requirements: Two or three courses from at least two different disciplines.

Choose from:

  • HIS 101 - World History I (3 credits)
  • HIS 102 - World History II (3 credits)
  • PHI 101 - Introduction to Philosophy (3 credits)
  • PHI 201 - Ethics (3 credits)
  • LIT 201 - World Literature (3 credits)
  • ART 101 - Art Appreciation (3 credits)
  • MUS 101 - Music Appreciation (3 credits)
  • THE 101 - Introduction to Theatre (3 credits)

Learning Outcomes: Engage with creative expression, historical contexts, philosophical inquiry, and aesthetic traditions.

6. Global & Cultural Awareness (3-6 credits)

Choose from:

  • SPA 101/102 - Elementary Spanish I/II (3-6 credits)
  • FRE 101/102 - Elementary French I/II (3-6 credits)
  • GLS 201 - Global Studies (3 credits)
  • REL 201 - World Religions (3 credits)
  • ANT 201 - Cross-Cultural Communication (3 credits)

Learning Outcomes: Develop intercultural competence, recognize global interconnectedness, and appreciate diverse perspectives.

Additional Requirements for Bachelor's Degrees

Students pursuing bachelor's degrees must also complete:

  • Upper-Level General Education: At least 6 credits of general education courses at the 300-400 level;
  • Writing Intensive Course: At least one designated writing-intensive course beyond ENG 102;
  • Ethics or Diversity Requirement: One course addressing ethical reasoning or diversity/inclusion.

Substitutions and Waivers

Students who have demonstrated proficiency through standardized testing (e.g., AP, CLEP, IB) may receive credit or waivers for general education courses. See the Transfer Credit Policy for details.

Requests for course substitutions must be approved by the academic advisor and the Registrar's Office.

Transfer Student Considerations

Transfer students who have completed an associate degree or 60+ credits at another regionally accredited institution may have met general education requirements if their previous institution's curriculum is deemed equivalent. Individual course evaluations are conducted by the Registrar's Office.
